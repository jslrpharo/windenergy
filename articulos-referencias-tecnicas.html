Selected publications, conference papers and training activities from ACMSL, documenting experience with wind turbine simulation training, industrial instrumentation, and professional development programmes.

10-1  AWEA 2016 – Better Wind Turbine Performance through Continuous Training

Conference: American Wind Energy Association (AWEA) WindPower 2016 Conference & Exhibition, New Orleans, Louisiana, USA.

Title: “Better Wind Turbine Performance through Continuous Training of Operations and Maintenance Personnel”

This paper presents the case for continuous, simulation-based training of O&M personnel as a key factor in improving wind turbine availability and reducing unscheduled maintenance costs. Key arguments include:

  • The gap between initial training and real-world operational competence
  • How real-time simulators reproduce fault scenarios that are rare in normal operation but critical to master
  • The use of ACMSL simulators at US technical colleges and European training centres
  • Measurable improvements in diagnostic speed after simulator-based training

10-2  Techwindgrid 2009 – Experience of Using High-Performance Dataloggers in Wind Turbine Maintenance

Conference: Techwindgrid 2009.

Title: “Experiencia ganada con el uso de un datalogger de altas prestaciones en aerogeneradores” (Experience gained using a high-performance datalogger in wind turbines)

This paper documents the field experience accumulated with the ACMSL DL1300 industrial datalogger deployed in real wind turbine installations. Topics covered include:

  • Challenges of acquiring multi-channel data from RS232/RS485 instrumentation in field conditions
  • Design decisions in the DL1300: XML configuration, fault tolerance, millisecond timestamping
  • Case studies: identifying hydraulic system degradation from logged data, detecting intermittent communication faults
  • Data volume management: selective sampling strategies for analogue vs. digital signals

10-3  Training for Wind Maintenance Team – Greenko Group (India)

ACMSL delivered a simulation-based training programme for the wind turbine operations and maintenance team of Greenko Group, one of India's leading renewable energy companies.

The programme covered:

  • Introduction to the electrical and mechanical architecture of DFIG wind turbines
  • Hands-on use of the ACMSL DFIG real-time simulator for fault scenario training
  • Controller incidence messages: mapping alarms to root causes and maintenance actions
  • Data analysis: using recorded signal data for performance monitoring and fault diagnosis

10-4  2022–2025 – Technical Teacher's Training Programs (Spain)

Between 2022 and 2025, ACMSL participated in multiple Formación del Profesorado de Formación Profesional (Vocational Training Teacher Development) — also known as Training for Trainers — programmes organised by regional education authorities across Spain, including:

  • Junta de Andalucía – Centros del Profesorado
  • Junta de Castilla y León – Centro de Formación del Profesorado
  • Comunidad de Madrid – Centro de Formación del Profesorado
  • Junta de Extremadura – Centro de Formación del Profesorado

These programmes trained vocational education teachers in the use of ACMSL wind turbine simulators as classroom tools, enabling them to deliver practical, hands-on wind energy training within their Vocational Training (FP) programmes.
