Wind Farms as Suppliers to the Electric Grid

Wind farms are the primary production units of wind energy, supplying generated power directly to the Transmission Network. This section covers the SCADA control centre, grid codes, and the real-time simulator that lets trainees explore every component of a wind farm.

  • Overall architecture of a wind farm (turbines, collection grid, substation)
  • SCADA functionalities: monitoring, alarm management, setpoint control
  • Communication architecture between the control centre and individual turbines
  • Meteorological tower and wind resource assessment
  • Power curve monitoring and performance analysis

2-2  The Electric Grid as Client of Wind Farms

The Transmission System Operator (TSO) imposes strict technical requirements on all generators connected to the grid. For wind farms, the most relevant grid characteristics are:

  • Grid Codes – minimum technical requirements for reactive power, voltage support, and fault ride-through (FRT/LVRT) capability.
  • Frequency regulation – contribution of wind farms to primary and secondary frequency control.
  • Voltage & reactive power – wind turbines must participate in voltage regulation at the point of common coupling (PCC).
  • Power quality – harmonics, flicker, and unbalance limits imposed by grid operators.

The ACMSL tutorial covers the fundamental characteristics of the Electric Grid that directly impact wind farm operation and grid compliance.
